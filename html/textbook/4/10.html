Advanced Lesson 10

Fate

Phrasal Verbs

get off / bury in sit around / run through

get off
to finish work, or have a break from work
bury in
to be so overwhelmed by work or a task that one feels completely covered up by it
turn someone off
to make someone feel that they are no longer interested in something to make someone feel that they are not attracted to you
sit around
to do nothing and be generally lazy; to procrastinate
run through
to read or rehearse without pausing

Words / Expressions

Directions : Listen and repeat after your teacher.

grab a bite to get something to eat; to get food that can be eaten quickly
perspective a way of thinking about something, especially one which is influenced by the type of person you are or by your experiences
fate a power that is believed to control what happens in people's lives
bookworm someone who likes reading very much
alchemist A person who studied the science of alchemy in the middle ages, which involved trying to change ordinary metals into gold
gist the main idea and meaning of what someone has said or written

Short Conversation

Directions : Read the following conversation with your teacher.

Allison came across Ken at the library where he works.
Allison Hey Ken! What are you doing here?
Ken I just got off my part time job. I work as a receptionist here.
How are you?
Allison Exhausted. I had to bury myself in tasks all day today, so I'm pretty tired. But hey, it's nice to see you again, Mr. Receptionist Do you wanna grab a bite?
Ken Sure. I'm pretty hungry. Plus, I was just gonna go home and sit around doing nothing, so why not?
Allison Cool. So here comes a million dollar question, what brought you to America?
Ken Oh, it's a long story, so I'll tell you about that some other time. But I have a better question for you. Why the Middle East, Allison?
Page 3
Allison Hmm, why did I go to the Middle East? That's a good question. I don't really know why, actually. One day, my step brother gave me this book that he was running through, and told me to read it. So I read it, and the book taught me many things about life, and somehow gave me a new broad perspective. And for the next moment, I pretty much found myself being in the Middle East, so it's what I call fate.
Ken What's the name of the book? As a bookworm, I'm very curious.
Allison It's called "Alchemist". I'm sure you are good at grasping the gist of a book, so I won't tell you what the book is about.

Question

Directions : Answer the questions about the story.

  1. Summarize the story using your own words.
  2. How do you think Ken felt about Allison's story?
