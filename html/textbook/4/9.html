Advanced Lesson 09

Blast

Phrasal Verbs

bring up / bring in turn off / knock out

bring up
to raise a new topic for discussion, or to mention a particular subject or issue in a conversation
bring in
to call people and have them join something such as a plan or a project
turn someone off
to make someone feel that they are no longer interested in something to make someone feel that they are not attracted to you
pull into
If a train, a truck or a car pulls in, it arrives somewhere.
knock out
to lose consciousness because you are hit so hard to impress someone very much with something you do, or something you make
*Knock yourself out = go for it

Short Conversation

Directions : Read the following conversation with your teacher.

James and Andrew are talking about Ken's birthday.
Andrew Hey, I heard Ken turned twenty-one a couple of months back in Japan.
James Yeah, he did. But when I asked him about his birthday, he said he only ate a cake.
Layla Yeah, yeah. My folks are good people, but whenever you decide to move out, you know you're more than welcome to stay with us.
Andrew What? No way, that's crazy. He only ate a piece of cake on his 21st birthday! What is he, a saint or something?
James Well, people are considered adults when they turn 20 in Japan, so I guess that's why he didn't have a blast on his 21st birthday.
Andrew I see. But the thing is, he is now with us in the States. #You know what I'm saying?
#An American expression asking if someone agrees with you.
James Oh, yeah, we have an obligation to throw an #epic burst for him, right?
Andrew Here we go, now we're talking. I'm glad I brought this up, buddy. We should bring in all homeys, and make sure everyone gets tipsy.
# 'Homeys' means 'friends' but is a very slang term only used in the U.S. by a certain demographic. It should not be used by foreigners. Most Americans never use this term.
