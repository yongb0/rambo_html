Advanced Lesson 12

Overloaded task

Phrasal Verbs

keep up / turn in / stay up / scare off / help out

keep up
if you keep up with someone, you stay at the same level and don't fall behind them
turn in
to submit something such as documents and assignments
stay up
to not go to bed at the time you would normally go to bed
scare off
to make someone uncertain or worried so that they do not do something they were going to do
help out
to help someone because they are busy or have problems

Words / Expressions

Directions : Listen and repeat after your teacher.

anatomy the scientific study of the structure of human or animal bodies
adolescent a young person, usually between the ages of 12 and 18, who is developing into an adult
overwhelm if someone is overwhelmed by an emotion, they feel it so strongly that they cannot think clearly
elective course that students can choose to take, but they do not have to take it in order to graduate
overloaded to give someone too much work or information to deal with
syllabus a plan that states exactly what students at a school or college should learn in particular subject

Short Conversation

Directions : Read the following conversation with your teacher.

Ken and his new friend Natalie are talking after history class.
Natalie So, what other classes are you taking this semester?
Ken I'm taking sociology, anatomy, literature, organic chemistry, and adolescent psychology.
Natalie Is sociology an elective class?
Ken Yeah, it is. And I heard anatomy and adolescent psychology were #the killer. Is it true?
#very difficult sometimes 'killer' means 'great', but not in this conversation
Natalie I've taken anatomy, and yes, the lecturer for that class was extremely strict and tough.
Ken How so? I'm already worried about that class because the professor speaks so fast, and I'm not sure if I can keep up with him.
Layla Hmm. James and Andrew are plotting a huge party for Ken I don't know how it will turn out but we're all going to the beach on the next weekend.
Natalie First, he'll give you lots of assignments and reading to do.
Then he'll make you write papers to turn in. It's overloaded.
I remember staying up all night writing the research papers for the class.
