Advanced Lesson 11

Chitchat

Phrasal Verbs

lure away / freak out / spit out / turn out

lure away
to persuade someone to do something, especially something wrong or dangerous, by making it seem attractive or exciting
freak out
to become very anxious, upset or afraid, or make someone very anxious, upset or afraid
spit out
to ask someone to tell you something that they seem too frightened or embarrassed to say
turn out
to happen in a particular way, or to have a particular result, especially one that you did not expect

Short Conversation

Directions : Read the following conversation with your teacher.

Layla went over to her older sister Sarah's house to talk.
Layla Where are my precious nephew and niece, Sarah?
Sarah Michael lured them away, don't worry.
Layla Hahaha lured them away, huh? Does he have any bait?
Sarah He always does, ice cream for today. So what's on your mind I got freaked out when you texted me this morning.
Layla What? Relax. I don't have anything to spit out, I'm just here for a chitchat.
Sarah Oh, really? Thank god.
