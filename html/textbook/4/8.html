Phrasal Verbs

sign up / take off / come back hang out / pick up

sign up
to put your name on a list for something because you want to take part in it
take off
to have a period of time away from work or school
come back
to return to a place
hang out
to spend time in a certain place, or with certain people
pick up
to meet people somewhere in order to give them a lift somewhere else

Words / Expressions

Directions : Listen and repeat after your teacher.

carrier company that provides a service such as insurance or telephones.
preference if you have a preference for something, you like it more than another thing and will choose it if you can.
adventurous not afraid of taking risks or trying new things.
spontaneous someone who is spontaneous does things without planning them first because they suddenly want to do it.
errand a short journey undertaken in order to do something for someone, for example delivering or collecting something for them.

Short Conversation

Directions : Read the following conversation with your teacher.

Charlotte stopped by her parents’ house to talk to Ken for a little while.
Charlotte What's your plan for today, Ken?
Ken Not much. James is taking me to the cell phone store so that I can sign up for a contract.
Charlotte Oh, if you don't have a carrier preference, why don't you join our family plan?
Ken Really? Sure, I appreciate the offer.
Charlotte Come on! We are family, remember? Here, give this paper to the cell phone people and you should be good to go.
Ken Thanks Charlotte. Are you going somewhere today?
Charlotte Yeah, that's why I'm here actually. I'm going to see my friend Allison. She took a year off from school and was living in the Middle East, but she came back recently.
Ken Wow! She seems like an adventurous person.
Charlotte Yes, indeed. She is definitely one of the most spontaneous friends I have, so I was wondering if you want to hang out with us.
