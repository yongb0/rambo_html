Advanced Lesson 06

Train Manners

Phrasal Verbs

get on / get off put on / give up

get on
to step onto a bus, train, ship, etc.
get off
to leave a means of transport such as a bus or a train
put on
to place one object on another
give up
to stop trying to do something for some reasons

Words / Expressions

Directions : Listen and repeat after your teacher.

insane completely stupid or crazy, often in a way that is dangerous
etiquette the formal rules for polite behavior in society or in a particular group
lap the upper part of your legs when you are sitting down
cramp a cramped room, building etc, does not have enough space for the people in it
elder people who are older than you whom you should respect
feast a very good large meal

Short Conversation

Directions : Read the following conversation with your teacher.

Emily was invited to the small family gathering at Layla's parents' house.
Emily When I think of Japan, the insane train video I watched on Youtube comes to my mind. Is it true that there are so many rules that you have to follow for the train ride?
Ken I can't think of so many rules, but there are certainly a few manners to keep, or they are more likely etiquettes.
Emily Tell me about them because I'm eager to know.
Ken Um,okay. First, when you get on a train, you have to let people who are getting off a train go first. Second, you have to hold your luggage somewhere in front of you.
Emily Yeah, I've seen people putting their bags on their laps in the video. The train looked so cramped though. Especially the crowds during rush hour seemed insane.
Ken so true, women sometimes get molested in a crowded train. Poor thing. Anyway, back to the story, you should also give up your seat for elders. And set your cell phone to silent mode, and refrain from talking on a train.
