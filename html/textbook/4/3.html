Words / Expressions

Directions : Listen and repeat after your teacher.

pregnancy when a woman is pregnant (=has a baby growing inside her body)
morning sickness a feeling of sickness that some women have during the morning when they are pregnant.
What are you having? asking about the baby's gender
(=If it's a boy or a girl)
delivery room a room in a hospital in which babies are born
give birth produces a baby or young animal from her body, bear a child or young
speaking of while we are talking about a particular person or thing

Short Conversation

Directions : Read the following conversation with your teacher.

Lydia ran into Olivia, mother of Layla, Sarah and Charlotte; at the grocery store.
Lydia Hello, Mrs. Turner. How are you doing?
Olivia Lydia, I'm doing great. Oh hey, congratulations on your pregnancy! Sarah told me about you the other day.
How are you feeling?
Lydia I've been fighting off my morning sickness. It sometimes gets pretty heavy on me, but other than that I'm doing fine.
I mean, "WE" are doing fine.
Olivia That's good to hear. Have you found out what you're having yet?
Lydia Not yet, but my husband and I will be grateful as long as the baby is healthy.
Olivia Of course. Looking back on my pregnancy with Sarah, I remember telling my doctor, who is my brother in law, not to tell me and my husband the sex. But we wanted a girl anyway.
Lydia Oh really, that's sweet. Was Mr. Turner in the delivery room when you gave birth to Sarah?
