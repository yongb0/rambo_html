Phrasal Verbs

drop by / cut down / work out go on / run into

drop by
to make a short, casual visit somewhere
cut down
to reduce the amount, number or size of something
work out
to do physical exercise to keep fit and healthy
go on
to keep happening as before, or to keep doing something to start a structured activity such as a diet or workout regimen
run into
to meet someone by chance, or without expecting to

Words / Expressions

coast the area where the land meets the sea

got to have got to
as right as rain to be healthy, especially after having been ill for a period of time
consume to eat or drink something / to use time, energy, goods, etc.
blood pressure the force with which blood travels through your body

Short Conversation

Directions : Read the following conversation with your teacher.

Olivia's brother Benjamin caught Daniel walking out the door on Sunday.
Benjamin Hey, Daniel. Where are you going all by yourself on this beautiful Sunday, huh?
Daniel Hey, Ben. I was about to go to take a walk on the coast.
Do you want to join me?
Benjamin Sure. I was dropping by to say hello to you all anyway.
So, how's it going?
Daniel Oh, not much. I had an annual health checkup and my doctor told me to cut down on drinking.
Benjamin Oh, you got to do what the doctor tells you, then you'll be as right as rain, you know? Speaking of which, I've been feeling well since I started working out again.
Daniel Really? That's good. Did your doctor tell you to go on a diet, too?
Because you actually look much healthier than the last time I ran into you at the hardware store about a month back.
