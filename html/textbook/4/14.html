Leisure

Phrasal Verbs

stick out / set up / loosen up / cry out

stick out
if something sticks out, you notice it because part of it comes out further than the rest of a surface
set up
to make the necessary arrangements for an event or activity
loosen up
to stop worrying and become less tense, less formal, or less restrained, and assume a friendlier manner
scare off
to make someone uncertain or worried so that they do not do something they were going to do
cry out
to shout or say something loudly

Directions : Read the following conversation with your teacher.

Layla, Charlotte, and Sarah's father Daniel is talking to his neighbor Nick.
Daniel Hey Nick, you've been golfing a lot lately, #huh? I see your long iron sticking out of your bag.

Nick Good morning, Mr. Turner. Yeah, I've been golfing ever since I went to the driving range with my friends a few months ago.
Daniel That's good. Do you ever play golf with your publishers?
Nick Yes, sometimes. They sneakily set up an appointment whenever they want to edit my literature, or negotiate something.
Daniel That's actually a good strategy though. I've read an article saying people often loosen up while they play golf.
Nick I think it's true. Some studies also show that crying out things like, "Fore!" or "You hit #the green!" also reduce stress levels. # 'The green' is the area around the targeted hole in golf
Daniel I agree on that theory. By the way, I heard you do winter sports and water sports, too. Is that right, Nick?
Nick Yes, I do. I'm a person with a wide range of interests, so I pretty much try everything.
