Midle Lesson 01

Have you ever been to France?

Today's GOAL

To be able to talk about your personal experiences.
自身の経験について話せるようになる。

Today's Phrase

Have you ever been to France?
Yes, I have. I've been there before.

Present Perfect is often used to talk about actions which happened at unspecified times before now. The exact time is not important. You cannot use Present Perfect with specific time expressions such as: yesterday, one year ago, last week, when I was a child, when I lived in Hawaii, at that moment, that day, etc. But, you can use Present Perfect with unspecific expressions such as: ever, never, once, many times, several times, before, already, yet, etc.
Today, we will learn one of the most common topics of present perfect, which is Experience. We often use present perfect to talk about personal experiences, just like the phrases above.

Page 1
Present perfect form >>>> have / has ＋ past participle (＝過去分詞)
Ex : I have seen that movie. / He has visited France.
Present perfect form >>>> Have / Has ＋ subject ＋ already ＋ past participle ?
Ex : Have you already taken a shower?

Short Conversation

Directions : Read the following conversation with your teacher.

Layla is looking for Emily and asking Ryan where she is.
Layla Ryan, have you seen Emily?
Ryan Yeah. She went to the movie with Aidan.
Layla That's sweet. Which movie did they go watch?
Ryan I don't remember the name of the movie, but it's a new French movie.
Layla Oh, I think I've seen it. Catherine Deneuve is in it, right?
Ryan I think that's it. She is the famous actress in Belle de jour, right?
Layla Yeah, that's her, and Belle de jour was such a masterpiece.
Ryan I'm not really a movie person, but even I know that one.
Layla Oh, I'm sure you do. I really loved the new movie too, I love French movies.
Ryan Have you ever been to France?
Layla Yes, I've been there before. It was a fascinating trip.
