Middle Lesson 55

Not many students flunked.

Today's GOAL

To be able to master many different ways of using "not"
"not" のさまざまな使い方をマスターできるようになる。

Today's Phrases

Not many students flunked.

Are you doing well?
If not, I can help you with that.

Not ～ (Negative sentence)

  1. Everyone had to hand in their paper by Friday, but not all the students finished it on time.
  2. Are you doing well in school? If not, I can help you with that. If not = If you are not doing well at school… (I can help you with that).

Short Conversation

Directions: Read the following conversation with your teacher.

Charlotte found David sitting on the bench by the fountain on the campus.
Charlotte Hello Mr. Romantic, are you waiting for my sister? May I sit?
David Yeah, and please go ahead and sit down. So, how are you doing? I heard you were doing really well in the graduate program, and also working as a TA (Teacher Assistant).
Charlotte Well, it's been a bit hectic since I had to grade the students’ papers, but I think I've been managing everything pretty well.
David Grading papers, huh? Are you failing many students?
Charlotte Hahaha, not really. Not all the students handed in their paper, but not many students flunked, I think.
David That's good. Sitting on this bench reminds me of many things.
Charlotte Right. How about you, David? Are you successfully persuading your scientific loving girlfriend to take it easy with her research? If not, I can always help you with that.
David Hahaha! Thanks, Charlotte! That's sweet. But Layla seems pretty enthusiastic about her work so I will wait it out. Meanwhile, I'll focus on my own career.

Exercise

Directions: Complete the sentences using Not ～＋ given words.

Example : Students had to hand in the paper by Friday, but not everyone finished it on time. (finish)
  1. Although Pablo Picasso is a talented artist, . (inspire)
    not everyone is inspired by his work.
  2. He joined their team at the recommendation of the coach, but . (welcome)
    not many people welcomed him.
  3. We should remember importance of good health . (*maintain = 維持する)
    not everyone maintains themselves.
  4. You are so strong that you can lift 1000 pounds, but . (*squeeze = 絞る)
    not everyone can squeeze as much as you.
  5. She should be thankful for her parents because . (*accomplish = 成し遂げる)
    not everyone has such accomplished parents.

Exercise

Directions: Complete the sentences using if not ～

Example : Have you been eating well to recover from the cold? If not, I can come to your apartment and cook for you.
  1. Is she successfully convincing your father to give us permission to go on a road trip?
    If not, I can try to help convince him.
  2. Have you found the store you were asking about the other day?
    If not, I can help you find it.
  3. A city bus should come to the bus station in about ten minutes.
    If not, I can come and give you a ride.
  4. Do you still have enough energy to take your dogs to the park?
    If not, I can take them to the park for you.
  5. Will he be able to give a presentation and have the clients sign for the contract?
    If not, we are going to be in trouble.
