Middle Lesson 49

I wish you had told me about it earlier.

Today's GOAL

To be able to express wishes and desires over things that didn't happen the way you wanted them to.
あなたの望みどおりに起きなかった願いや欲求を表現できるようになる。

Today's Phrases

I wish you had told me about that earlier.
If only I had known that you wanted them.

Wish / If only + had Vpp

We used “wish / if only “ to talk about our desire which didn't happen the way we wanted.

I wish I had been married to Cameron Diaz. = If only I had been married to Cameron Diaz.

>> This person didn't get married to Cameron Diaz, but he is almost daydreaming "If I had been married to Cameron, something would, might, could, must have been different."

Short Conversation

Directions: Read the following conversation with your teacher.

Andrew comes home and is having a little chat with his roommate, Elijah.
Andrew Hey buddy! How's it going? I like your new shoes. Where did you get them?
Elijah Not bad, thanks. I got them from the new sports shop in the mall This new material absorbs sweat more quickly than the material used in their old shoes, isn't that neat?
Andrew For real? I went to the mall to buy tennis shoes yesterday, as well. That's exactly what I wanted. I wish you had told me about those shoes. If you had, I would have bought them myself, too.
Elijah Really. What happened to the ones you had? They weren't that old.
Andrew I went to visit my parents this weekend, and our dog chewed up my shoes. He occasionally does that whenever he wants more attention.
Elijah That's bad. If only I had known that you wanted them, I could have bought two pairs and sold one of them to you and double the price.
Andrew You are more evil than my dog, aren't you? Hahahaha.

Exercise

Directions: Complete the sentences using should have.

Example : I wish I had known about the new products. I should have asked you earlier. ( ask )
  1. I wish I had gone to the party with you. . ( call )
    I should have called you.
  2. If only I had not eaten at that terrible restaurant. . ( stay )
    I should have stayed home.
  3. I wish he had made it on time. . ( drive )
    He should have driven faster.
  4. If only she had not read that letter. . ( *throw away = 捨てる )
    She should have thrown it away.

Exercise

Directions: Make sentences using would have, might have, or could have..

Example : I wish you had told me about the new shoes. If you had, I would definitely have bought those shoes, too. ( would have )
  1. If only you had told me that you wanted my watch. If you had, . ( could have )
    I could have kept it for you.
  2. He wishes he had known that she was rich. If he had, . ( might have )
    he might have borrowed some money from her.
  3. If only I had not known the fact that he's married. If I hadn't, . ( would have )
    I would have asked him out on a date.
