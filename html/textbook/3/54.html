Since we have learned about the basic usage of NOT (such as "I don't ～ / They haven't ～ ",) we will now move on to the next level and learn a more advanced usage of NOT.

I got fired for not being punctual. / I worked hard not to be financially dependent.

Exercise

Directions: Complete the sentences using not ＋ adverb (in the box)

always （常に） necessarily （必ず） exactly （正確に）completely （完全に）
absolutely （ 絶対に） entirely （全く）

Example: The couples are holding hands and smiling at each other, but they are not always happy.

Always not happy = Always unhappy
Not always happy = Sometimes unhappy
  1. Although the construction work seems all done, it's ________ done yet.
    not entirely
  2. We all think teachers are always right, but it's _________________ true.
    not necessarily
  3. The weathercaster is ____________ sure that there will be a large amount of snow next week.
    not absolutely
  4. Although his speech was very passionate, it was ____________ convincing.
    not completely
  5. You may think your proposition is perfect, yet it's ________________ right.
    not exactly

Exercise

Directions: Change the sentences into their the passive forms.

Example: Gabriella got fired for not being punctual.
She worked hard not to be financially dependent.
  1. He tried ________________________________________________ .
    not to get in trouble.
  2. That house is ____________________________________________.
    famous for being very expensive.
  3. I asked her ______________________________________________ .
    not to be late.
  4. She was nominated ________________________________________ .
    for not ever missing a day of work.
  5. They had to agree with them in order ___________________________.
    not to cause problems.
