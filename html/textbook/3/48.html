Middle Lesson 48

He must have made an excuse for himself.

Today's GOAL

To be able to express possibilities in the past.
過去の可能性を言及できるようになる。

Today's Phrase

He must have made an excuse for himself.
He might have really gone to the hospital.
Perfect Modals ( Must have Vpp / Might have Vpp )

"Must (not) have"... Possibility that probably happened or didn't happen in the past.
He must have lied to her. (You strongly believe that he lied to her.)
"Might (not) have"... Possibility that maybe happened or didn't happen in the past.
He might have lied to her. (You are not sure whether he lied to her or not.)

Short Conversation

Directions: Read the following conversation with your teacher.

David and his coworker are talking about another coworker, Charlie.
Lisa Charlie is still not here. He told me he was going to the hospital before work, but he must have lied. It's probably just a fake illness.
David Why would you say that?
Lisa I don't know, maybe because I've never seen him being serious about anything.
David Yeah. He's goofy, but he might have really gone to the hospital. You never know!
Lisa The first time I met him, he was making a dirty joke to the manager's wife. Can you believe that? I've thought he's the kind of person who would deceive the elderly into paying more than the listed price for jewelry ever since.
David Hahaha! What a vivid imagination! But he's not like that! He was probably just trying to be friendly with the manager's wife. I believe he is honest.
Lisa But his reputation says otherwise. I think he's a dishonest person.

Exercise

Directions: Answer the questions using might have Vpp.

Example : Do you think John went to work today? I don't know. He might have gone there.
  1. Do you think he got the *scholarship? （ *scholarship = 奨学金） .
    He might have got / gotten the scholarship.
  2. Do you think she saw the article about herself in the newspaper this morning? .
    She might have seen the article.
  3. Do you think they wrote a letter to the company? .
    He might have quit smoking.
  4. Do you think those children already ate *supper? （ *supper = 夕食） .
    They might have already eaten supper.

Exercise

Directions: Make sentences using must have Vpp.

Ex : Tom looked unhappy because he had lost his cellphone, but now he's smiling.
>> He must have found his cellphone.
  1. Lucy loves her husband and never complains unless he gives her some strange gifts. Uh oh, she's complaining now.
    >> .
    He must have given her some strange gifts.
  2. Jacob promised to show us his new computer today, but he doesn't have the computer.
    >> .
    He must have forgotten it at home.
  3. There was some milk in the refrigerator. Now it's all gone, and there's a milk *mustache ( = 口髭) around his mouth.
    >> .
    He must have drunk all the milk.
  4. According to Naomi, her TV has been acting weirdly. Now she's throwing away the TV.
    >> .
    It must have been broken.
  5. There's no food in the house. Craig has just left the house with his wallet.
    >> .
    He must have left to a supermarket.
