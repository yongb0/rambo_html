[ wt_style_6 ]

Native's Mind

『My name is 』も『I'm 』も、どちらも『私の名前は / 私はです』という表現ですが、『My name is 』の方が少し硬いイメージがあるので、ネイティブスピーカーは日常会話では『I'm 』の方を良く使います。英語ではこのイメージがとても大事になります。

Phrasal Verbs

[ wt_style_12 .words ]

come along / run away / drop off / pull into / fill up

There are more meanings for some of these than what is listed here, but we are introducing them gradually so as not to overwhelm the student.

come along
to make progress or to improve in some way
run away
to leave home permanently, with the intention of never returning, without telling one’ s parents or guardians
drop off
to drive someone to a place they need to go to and leave them there
pull into
If a train, a truck or a car pulls in, it arrives somewhere
fill up
to make something full

[ wt_style_13 ]

folks parents
pump to make liquid or gas move in a particular direction, using a pump
self-serve gas station gas station where you pump your own gas.
obnoxious very offensive, unpleasant, or rude
gouge to charge excessive prices for something / to make a deep hole or cut in the surface of something
shortage a situation in which there is not enough of something that people need
